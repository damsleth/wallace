# T6040 J614s audio topology inventory — ticket 040 (2026-07-23)

Inventory of the J614s audio ADT (`/arm-io`, from the captured
`j614s-usb-port-map-20260721.adt` via m1n1 `adt.py`). Stage G, upstream-tracked,
**not boot-critical**; this maps the parts so the eventual macaudio DT wiring is
grounded and the driver-gap is known.

## Parts

| Role | Part (ADT compatible) | Bus / addr |
|---|---|---|
| Speaker amps (×6) | `audio-control,sn012776` (TI) | i2c1: left woofer1 `0x38`, woofer2, tweeter; i2c3: right woofer1 `0x3b`, woofer2, tweeter |

Speaker array: **2 woofers + 1 tweeter per side (stereo, 6 drivers)** — a
`speakersafetyd`-gated multiway config.

## Driver-support assessment (vs upstream Asahi)

1. **`sn012776` is the M4-generation TI amp** — newer than the `tas2764`/`tas2770`
   on M1/M2 Macs. Upstream `tas2764`/`tas27xx` + the macaudio machine driver may
   not yet bind `sn012776`; likely needs a compatible add / small driver delta.
   Track upstream; do not fork here.
2. **`cs42l84`** — supported by the Asahi `cs42l84` driver (M2/M3-era jack codec);
   should bind with correct DT wiring.
3. **Transport is the AOP `iop-audio-controller` (mca/leap ADMA)** — M4 routes
   audio through the AOP audio service, similar to recent Apple Silicon. The
   macaudio/`apple,mca` + `admac` stack and its M4/AOP variant must be present
   upstream; this is the load-bearing enablement item.
4. **Speaker safety is a hard gate**: `sn012776` playback needs `speakersafetyd`
   limits **and an asahi-audio DSP profile measured for this exact J614s chassis**
   — which will not exist upstream until someone measures it. Until then speakers
   stay muted; **headphones (cs42l84) and USB/DP audio work far earlier.**

## Conclusion / next

The map is complete. Audio remains Stage G / upstream-track: the enabling work
is upstream (`sn012776` amp support, the M4 AOP-mca audio path, a j614s speaker
profile), not something to build on this rig. When macaudio lands M4 support,
the j614s DT needs: the 6 amps on i2c1/i2c3 at the addresses above, cs42l84 on
i2c2 `0x4b`, and the mca/admac wiring — all now inventoried. Headphone-out via
cs42l84 is the first realistic audio milestone; speakers wait on a measured
profile. No forbidden writes involved (all i2c/status). Ticket 040 done.
